Founding Document

The Constitutional Charter
of the Republic of Insulide

RATIFIED — 1 JANUARY 2024 — DOCUMENT REF: CONST-001-EN
Notice: This document is a placeholder. The full Constitutional Charter of the Republic of Insulide is currently being drafted by the Office of the Chancellor and will be published in its complete form upon ratification. The articles below represent the foundational framework as adopted at establishment.
We, the founding citizens of the Republic of Insulide, in recognition of the geographic fortune that places us at the crossroads of the world's commerce, and in pursuit of a society governed by reason, bound by law, and open to all who come in good faith — do hereby establish this Charter as the supreme law of our Republic.
Chapter I

The State & Its Sovereignty

ARTICLE 1

The Republic of Insulide is a sovereign, independent, and democratic state. Its sovereignty is absolute and indivisible. No external authority may legislate for, govern, or claim jurisdiction over the territory of Insulide without the express consent of its Parliament.

ARTICLE 2

The territory of Insulide comprises the island and all engineered extensions thereof, its territorial waters extending 12 nautical miles from the coastal baseline, and its exclusive economic zone of 200 nautical miles.

ARTICLE 3

The official languages of the Republic are English, French, and Russian. All laws, official documents, and government communications shall be published in all three languages. In the event of conflict between versions, the English text shall prevail.

Chapter II

Fundamental Rights

ARTICLE 4

All persons within the territory of Insulide are equal before the law. No person shall be discriminated against on the basis of origin, language, belief, or economic status.

ARTICLE 5

The right to property is guaranteed. No property shall be expropriated except for demonstrable public necessity and upon payment of fair and immediate compensation.

ARTICLE 6

Freedom of commerce is a constitutional right. No law may impose tariffs on goods in transit through the Port of Insulide. This provision may not be amended by ordinary parliamentary majority.

Chapter III

Governance & Parliament

ARTICLE 7

The Parliament of Insulide is housed within the Governance Tower and constitutes the supreme legislative authority of the Republic. It shall convene in permanent session and may not be prorogued for more than 14 consecutive days.

ARTICLE 8

Ministers are appointed on the basis of demonstrated expertise in their portfolio. Political affiliation shall not be a criterion for ministerial appointment or dismissal.

Chapter IV

Economic Provisions

ARTICLE 9

The corporate tax rate shall not exceed 8.5% of net profit. This rate is constitutionally fixed and may only be reduced, never increased, except by a referendum of the citizenry with no less than two-thirds participation.

ARTICLE 10

The Insulide Stock Exchange shall operate as an independent institution under statutory regulation. Its governance structure shall be free from direct parliamentary interference in its day-to-day operations.

Adopted and ratified by the Founding Assembly of the Republic of Insulide

1st January, 2024

The Chancellor of Insulide
