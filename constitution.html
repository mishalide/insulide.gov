Founding Document

The Constitutional Charter
of the Republic of Insulide

RATIFIED — 1 JANUARY 2024 — DOCUMENT REF: CONST-001-EN
Contents

The Republic of Insulide is founded upon the recognition that good governance is a technical and institutional problem, not merely a political one; that the prosperity of a people depends upon the quality of the rules under which they live; and that a small, well-ordered state, situated at the crossroads of global commerce, may achieve what larger and more diffuse polities cannot.

We, the founding citizens of Insulide, acting through the Constituent Assembly convened on this first day of January 2024, hereby ordain and establish this Charter as the supreme law of the Republic, binding upon all persons and all institutions within its territory and jurisdiction, without exception.

Chapter I

The State

ARTICLE 1Name and Form

The state is named the Republic of Insulide, hereinafter referred to as "the Republic" or "Insulide".

The Republic is a sovereign, unitary, democratic state governed in accordance with the rule of law and this Charter.

The sovereignty of Insulide is absolute and indivisible. No instrument of international law, treaty, or agreement shall be construed to derogate from the sovereignty of the Republic except where expressly ratified by the Parliament by a two-thirds majority and confirmed by referendum.

ARTICLE 2Territory

The territory of the Republic comprises:

  • The island of Insulide and all permanent engineered extensions thereof, including all present and future landfill;
  • Territorial waters extending twelve (12) nautical miles from the outermost point of the coastal baseline at mean low water;
  • The exclusive economic zone extending two hundred (200) nautical miles from the coastal baseline;
  • The airspace above all of the foregoing.

Any alteration to the boundaries of the Republic shall require the approval of the Parliament by a three-quarters majority.

ARTICLE 3Official Languages

The official languages of the Republic are English, French, and Russian.

All legislation, official publications, judicial decisions, and government communications shall be published in all three official languages simultaneously.

In the event of any inconsistency or conflict between official language versions of any legal text, the English language text shall be authoritative and shall prevail.

ARTICLE 4National Symbols

The Parliament shall, by ordinary legislation, establish the flag, coat of arms, and national anthem of the Republic.

The Republic recognises gold as the monetary standard of commerce within Insulide, consistent with prevailing convention.

Chapter II

Fundamental Rights and Freedoms

ARTICLE 5Equality Before the Law

All persons within the jurisdiction of the Republic are equal before the law and are entitled to equal protection and benefit of the law.

No person shall be subjected to discrimination by any public authority on grounds of national or ethnic origin, language, religion, sex, age, disability, or economic status.

The right to equality before the law shall be enforceable in the Constitutional Court.

ARTICLE 6Personal Liberty

Every person has the right to liberty and security of person.

No person shall be detained without charge for more than forty-eight (48) hours. Any extension of pre-charge detention shall require an order of a judge.

Every person charged with an offence is presumed innocent until proven guilty according to law in a fair and public hearing.

ARTICLE 7Freedom of Expression and Assembly

Every person has the right to freedom of expression, including freedom of the press and freedom of information.

Every person has the right to peaceful assembly and association.

These freedoms may be restricted only by law, only to the extent demonstrably necessary in a free and democratic society, and only for the purposes of protecting the rights of others, maintaining public order, or preserving national security.

ARTICLE 8Right to Property

Every person has the right to acquire, hold, and dispose of property.

No property shall be expropriated by any public authority except for a public purpose established by law, upon payment of fair and prompt compensation at market value, and subject to judicial review.

Intellectual property rights are guaranteed and shall be protected under law.

ARTICLE 9Freedom of Commerce

The freedom to engage in trade, commerce, and economic activity is a fundamental right of all persons within the Republic.

No law shall impose restrictions on the freedom of commerce except where strictly necessary and proportionate to a legitimate public interest.

The Parliament shall have no power to impose tariffs on goods in transit through the Port of Insulide. This provision shall not be subject to ordinary amendment and may be altered only pursuant to Article 37(2) of this Charter.

ARTICLE 10Right to Education and Health

Every citizen of the Republic has the right of access to a public education system of adequate quality.

The state shall ensure that basic healthcare is accessible to all residents of the Republic on an equitable basis.

The Parliament shall provide by law for the progressive realisation of these rights, subject to the available resources of the Republic.

Chapter III

The Parliament

ARTICLE 11Legislative Authority

The legislative authority of the Republic is vested in the Parliament of Insulide.

The Parliament shall be the supreme legislative body of the Republic and shall exercise its powers in accordance with this Charter.

The Parliament shall sit within the Governance Tower of Insulide and shall hold sessions throughout the year. The Parliament may not be prorogued for more than fourteen (14) consecutive days.

ARTICLE 12Composition

The Parliament shall consist of not fewer than thirty (30) and not more than sixty (60) members, as determined by the Electoral Act.

Members of Parliament shall be elected by universal, equal, direct, and secret ballot of citizens who have attained the age of eighteen (18) years.

The term of the Parliament is four (4) years from the date of the first sitting following a general election, subject to earlier dissolution under Article 20.

ARTICLE 13Qualifications and Disqualifications

A person is qualified for election to Parliament if they are a citizen of the Republic, have attained the age of twenty-one (21) years, and are not subject to any disqualification under this Article.

A person is disqualified from membership of Parliament if they:

  • Hold, or have within the preceding two years held, any undisclosed financial interest in a matter directly regulated by legislation under active consideration by the Parliament;
  • Have been convicted of an offence carrying a custodial sentence of more than twelve months;
  • Have been adjudicated insolvent and not discharged.
ARTICLE 14Speaker

The Parliament shall elect from among its members a Speaker and a Deputy Speaker, who shall preside over proceedings.

The Speaker shall be responsible for maintaining order in the Parliament, certifying legislation, and performing such other functions as prescribed by law.

The Speaker shall not vote in parliamentary proceedings except to cast a deciding vote in the event of a tie.

ARTICLE 15Legislative Procedure

Bills may be introduced by any member of Parliament or by the Chancellor on behalf of the Government.

A bill shall become law upon passing the Parliament by a simple majority of members present and voting, receiving the assent of the Chancellor, and being published in the Official Gazette.

If the Chancellor declines to assent to a bill, the Chancellor shall within seven (7) days return the bill to the Parliament with written reasons. The Parliament may pass the bill a second time by an absolute majority of all members, whereupon assent is deemed given.

ARTICLE 16Budget and Appropriations

No expenditure from public funds shall be made except pursuant to an appropriation authorised by the Parliament.

The Chancellor shall present the annual budget to the Parliament not less than thirty (30) days before the commencement of the financial year to which it relates.

If the Parliament does not pass an appropriations act before the commencement of the financial year, the Government may expend funds at a rate not exceeding the rate of the preceding year's appropriations, pending passage of the budget.

Chapter IV

The Chancellor and the Government

ARTICLE 17Executive Authority

The executive authority of the Republic is vested in the Chancellor of Insulide.

The Chancellor shall be responsible for the general direction and control of the Government, the implementation of legislation, and the conduct of foreign affairs.

The Chancellor and the Government are collectively responsible to the Parliament for the exercise of executive authority.

ARTICLE 18Appointment of the Chancellor

The Chancellor shall be the member of Parliament who commands the confidence of the majority of the Parliament.

Following a general election, or following a vote of no confidence, the Parliament shall elect a Chancellor by absolute majority within fourteen (14) days.

If no member obtains an absolute majority within fourteen (14) days, the Parliament shall be dissolved and a general election called.

ARTICLE 19Ministerial Appointments

The Chancellor shall appoint Ministers of the Government, who shall individually be responsible for the administration of the portfolios assigned to them.

Each Minister shall be appointed on the basis of demonstrated professional expertise in their portfolio area. Political affiliation shall not be a disqualifying factor, but shall not constitute a qualification in itself.

The number of Ministers shall not exceed fifteen (15) without amendment to this Article.

ARTICLE 20Vote of No Confidence and Dissolution

The Parliament may pass a vote of no confidence in the Chancellor by an absolute majority of all members.

Upon a successful vote of no confidence, the Chancellor shall resign and the Parliament shall proceed to elect a new Chancellor pursuant to Article 18.

The Chancellor may, at any time, advise the dissolution of the Parliament and the calling of a general election. A general election shall be held not later than sixty (60) days after dissolution.

Chapter V

Economic Governance

ARTICLE 21Economic Principles

The economy of the Republic shall be founded upon the principles of free markets, open trade, fiscal responsibility, and the rule of law.

The state shall provide a stable and predictable legal and regulatory environment conducive to investment, enterprise, and innovation.

The Parliament shall not enact legislation that creates an unnecessary impediment to economic activity without a compelling and proportionate public interest justification.

ARTICLE 22Corporate Taxation

The standard rate of corporate income tax shall not exceed zero per cent (0%) of net profit.

The state may, by legislation passed pursuant to the Investment Incentives Act, establish a negative effective corporate tax rate, expressed as a net credit payable by the state to qualifying enterprises, of up to five per cent (5%) of eligible capital expenditure per annum.

The rate specified in clause (1) of this Article may not be increased except by a referendum of the citizenry in accordance with Article 37(2), in which not fewer than two-thirds of all registered voters participate, and in which a majority vote in favour of the increase.

No retrospective increase in corporate tax shall be made. Any change in the rate shall apply prospectively only, with not less than five (5) years' notice to affected enterprises.

⚠ ENTRENCHED PROVISION — Amendment requires unanimous parliamentary vote + referendum. See Article 37.
ARTICLE 23Free Trade and Port Operations

The Port of Insulide shall operate as a free port. No tariff, duty, or levy shall be imposed on goods in transit through the Port.

The Parliament may impose reasonable port fees to cover the cost of port administration and infrastructure, provided such fees are non-discriminatory and publicly published.

Insulide shall seek, as a standing matter of foreign policy, to conclude free trade agreements with all willing partners. The Chancellor shall report annually to the Parliament on the status of all trade negotiations.

ARTICLE 24Budget and Fiscal Rules

The Government shall maintain the public finances of the Republic on a sound and sustainable basis.

The Parliament shall not appropriate expenditure that causes the annual fiscal deficit to exceed three per cent (3%) of gross domestic product in any financial year, except in the event of a declared national emergency.

The Government shall publish a medium-term fiscal plan covering a period of not less than five (5) years, updated annually alongside the budget.

ARTICLE 25The Insulide Stock Exchange

The Insulide Stock Exchange is hereby established as an independent statutory institution.

The Exchange shall operate under the supervision of the Insulide Financial Regulatory Authority, which shall be independent of the Government and shall report directly to the Parliament.

No Minister, member of Parliament, or public official shall hold a direct financial interest in any entity listed on the Exchange during the term of their appointment or election, subject to the interests disclosed in the Register of Interests.

ARTICLE 26Pharmaceutical and Advanced Manufacturing Sectors

The Parliament may, by legislation, establish special regulatory frameworks for the pharmaceutical, biotechnology, and advanced manufacturing sectors, including accelerated licensing pathways and investment incentives.

Any such framework shall be consistent with the Republic's international treaty obligations and shall not be used to facilitate the circumvention of legitimate intellectual property protections.

Chapter VI

The Judiciary

ARTICLE 27Judicial Independence

The judiciary of the Republic is independent of the executive and the legislature.

Judges shall hold office during good behaviour and shall not be removed except by address of the Parliament supported by a two-thirds majority, on grounds of incapacity or misconduct established by a judicial tribunal.

The remuneration of judges shall not be reduced during their term of office.

⚠ ENTRENCHED PROVISION — Amendment requires unanimous parliamentary vote + referendum. See Article 37.
ARTICLE 28Court Structure

The courts of the Republic shall consist of:

  • The Constitutional Court, which is the supreme court for all matters of constitutional law;
  • The Commercial Court, which shall have jurisdiction over commercial, corporate, and financial disputes;
  • The General Court, which shall have jurisdiction over all other civil and criminal matters;
  • Such other courts and tribunals as may be established by legislation.
ARTICLE 29The Constitutional Court

The Constitutional Court shall consist of five (5) judges appointed by the Parliament on the recommendation of an independent judicial appointments commission.

The Constitutional Court shall have exclusive jurisdiction to determine the constitutionality of any law, executive act, or decision of a public body.

Any person may apply to the Constitutional Court for relief where a right guaranteed by Chapter II of this Charter has been infringed or threatened.

ARTICLE 30Insulide Sovereign Jurisdiction

The courts of the Republic shall apply the law of Insulide, including this Charter, applicable legislation, and the common law of Insulide as developed by the courts.

The courts shall have regard to international law and the jurisprudence of foreign courts as a persuasive, but not binding, source of law.

No judgment of a foreign court shall be enforceable within the Republic without recognition by the courts of Insulide in accordance with the Private International Law Act.

⚠ ENTRENCHED PROVISION — Amendment requires unanimous parliamentary vote + referendum. See Article 37.
Chapter VII

Foreign Affairs and Sovereignty

ARTICLE 31Foreign Policy Principles

The foreign policy of the Republic shall be guided by the principles of sovereign equality, non-interference in the internal affairs of other states, peaceful resolution of disputes, and the promotion of free and open trade.

Insulide shall seek to maintain cordial and mutually beneficial relations with all states and international organisations.

The Republic shall maintain a lean but effective diplomatic presence in key commercial and diplomatic centres.

ARTICLE 32Treaty-Making

The power to conclude treaties and international agreements is vested in the Chancellor, acting on behalf of the Republic.

Any treaty that imposes financial obligations on the Republic, affects the rights of citizens, or modifies the domestic law of Insulide shall require ratification by the Parliament by a simple majority before coming into force.

No treaty shall be ratified that purports to cede, diminish, or impair the sovereignty of the Republic or the jurisdiction of its courts.

ARTICLE 33Defence and Security

The Parliament shall provide by law for the security and defence of the Republic, including the establishment of a coast guard and police service.

The use of force abroad shall not be authorised except in the exercise of the inherent right of self-defence under international law, and shall in all cases require the prior approval of the Parliament.

Chapter VIII

Public Administration

ARTICLE 34Principles of Public Administration

The public administration of the Republic shall be governed by the principles of legality, impartiality, transparency, accountability, and efficiency.

Appointments to the public service shall be made on merit. Political patronage in public appointments is prohibited.

Every person has the right of access to information held by any public body, subject only to such limitations as are necessary to protect legitimate state interests, the privacy of individuals, or the confidentiality of commercial information.

ARTICLE 35Register of Interests

The Parliament shall maintain a publicly accessible Register of Interests in which all members of Parliament, Ministers, and senior public officials shall disclose all financial interests, directorships, and significant assets within thirty (30) days of taking office and annually thereafter.

Failure to make a required disclosure, or the making of a false disclosure, shall be an offence punishable by removal from office and such further penalties as the Parliament may prescribe.

Chapter IX

Amendment

ARTICLE 36General Amendment Procedure

This Charter may be amended by a bill passed by the Parliament by a two-thirds majority of all members, followed by a referendum in which a majority of votes cast are in favour of the amendment.

No amendment may be put to referendum unless it has been published in the Official Gazette not less than ninety (90) days before the date of the referendum.

An amendment to this Charter shall come into force upon certification of the referendum result by the Electoral Commission.

ARTICLE 37Entrenched Provisions

The following provisions of this Charter are entrenched and shall not be subject to amendment under the general procedure in Article 36:

  • Article 9 (Freedom of Commerce), insofar as it prohibits tariffs on goods in transit;
  • Article 22 (Corporate Taxation), insofar as it establishes the zero per cent rate;
  • Article 27 (Judicial Independence);
  • Article 30 (Insulide Sovereign Jurisdiction);
  • Article 37 (this Article).

An entrenched provision may be amended only by a bill passed by a unanimous vote of all members of Parliament, followed by a referendum in which not fewer than two-thirds of all registered voters participate and a majority vote in favour of the amendment.

⚠ ENTRENCHED PROVISION — This Article is itself entrenched and subject to the same unanimous vote requirement.

This Charter was duly adopted by the Constituent Assembly of the Republic of Insulide,
meeting in full session within the Governance Tower,
on the first day of January, in the year two thousand and twenty-four.


Done in the English, French, and Russian languages, each version being equally authentic,
provided that in the event of inconsistency the English text shall prevail.
