Juice Capital Partners has formally expressed interest in acquiring Goober Island from the government of Debara as part of an ambitious environmental restoration and nation-building initiative. The firm has begun preliminary discussions with Debaran authorities regarding permission to redevelop the island, which falls within Debara's territorial jurisdiction.

While the proposal includes a major environmental cleanup and restoration program, company representatives say the long-term goal is significantly broader: the creation of a new self-governing economic zone built around principles of open markets, regulatory efficiency, and sustainable development.

Under the plan, Juice Capital Partners would first undertake a comprehensive environmental rehabilitation effort. This phase would focus on ecosystem restoration, shoreline stabilization, and the removal of any environmental degradation that currently affects the island. Company officials say this restoration effort is intended to create a stable ecological foundation before any permanent settlement or infrastructure development begins.

Following the restoration phase, the firm envisions the island evolving into a newly built city-state with a strong emphasis on economic openness and institutional experimentation. Early concept materials describe a governance model centered on streamlined regulatory frameworks, strong property rights protections, and policies designed to encourage international investment, entrepreneurship, and technological development.

Project organizers describe the concept as an opportunity to build modern civic and economic institutions from the ground up. Planned infrastructure would prioritize resilient energy systems, high-density walkable urban design, and digital-first governance systems intended to support a globally connected population of residents, businesses, and research organizations.

Negotiations with Debara would ultimately determine the island's legal status and degree of autonomy. Juice Capital Partners has indicated it is seeking an arrangement that would allow the territory to function with substantial administrative independence while maintaining a cooperative relationship with Debara.
